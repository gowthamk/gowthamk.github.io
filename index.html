I am a Ph.D student in Programming Languages and Software Engineering at Purdue CS. My advisor is Prof. Suresh Jagannathan. The focus of my work is on designing new programming language techniques to enhance software integrity and developer productivity. At Purdue, I built Catalyst, a dependently typed variant of ML that can automatically verify data structure invariants, and contributed to Quelea, a declarative programming framework for NoSQL applications. My research interests lie in type systems, program analyses, program synthesis, constraint solving, specification languages and axiomatic modeling of systems. I frequently blog my research notes here.

Before starting my Ph.D I was a front-end engineer at Yahoo! SDC, Bangalore, where I built publishing tools for Yahoo's editors. I graduated with B.E (Hons.) in Computer Science from BITS, Pilani, India in 2009.

I did my 2014 and 2015 summer internships at Microsoft Research India.
